Utilities & Infrastructure Owners

Water, district heating, wastewater, electricity, telecom — underground & above-ground networks
Danish Utility Regulator · Civil 3D · Georadar · GIS-BIM
Danish utility networks are largely invisible — buried infrastructure under roads, fields, and private land. Inaccurate or missing as-built records cause dangerous excavations, unplanned outages, and costly emergency repairs. Civil 3D infrastructure modelling, georadar surveys, and GIS-BIM integration transforms fragmented network records into reliable, spatially accurate operational data.
Critical Challenges
  • Underground utility records remain incomplete and fragmented, with many critical assets still based on legacy documentation that cannot be fully trusted.
  • The absence of integrated GIS and engineering data creates silos and prevents reliable network-wide decision-making.
  • GIS and engineering systems disconnected — no single source of truth for network
  • Inconsistent as-built handover standards reduce the value of project data and weaken lifecycle asset management.
  • Utility expansion projects, especially district heating and cooling, are often delivered without sufficient 3D coordination across adjacent infrastructure networks.
Operational KPIs
  • Spatial data accuracy across the asset network, measured by the share of assets verified within ±0.5 m tolerance.
  • Excavation strike frequency, expressed as incidents per 1,000 digs.
  • Emergency repair callouts vs planned maintenance
  • Field asset retrieval time, measuring how quickly crews can locate assets from GIS data.
  • Capital project data compliance, tracking the proportion of as-built records delivered in the required format and quality.

Business Value
  • Early-stage georadar (GPR) surveys provide verified identification and confidence classification of buried infrastructure prior to excavation, reducing utility strikes, service outages, safety incidents, and associated contractor claims, while establishing a reliable subsurface data foundation for future projects.
  • Standardised Civil 3D machine-control design profiles, delivered in accordance with defined ICT requirements, provide contractors with GPS-ready excavation models that reduce over-excavation, minimize material waste, and lower reinstatement and remediation costs, while ensuring consistent execution across capital projects.

Contractors

General contractors, MEP specialists, turnkey builders
ICT Order · EIR · Dalux Field
Contractors face mandatory ICT Order (IKT-bekendtgørelsen) compliance obligations from contract signature, yet most operate without a dedicated BIM or ICT lead — creating delivery risk, uncertainty in interpretation, and dependence on external expertise. Clashes discovered after subcontractor procurement, site teams on superseded drawings, and handover packages failing client audits are the direct consequences. On-demand BIM & ICT expertise covers the full delivery chain — from tender coordination through verified digital handover.
Specific Pain Points
  • Mandatory ICT Order compliance without an in-house BIM or ICT lead — creating delivery risk, interpretation uncertainty, and increased reliance on external consultants.
  • Clash detection performed too late in the project lifecycle, often after subcontractor procurement, resulting in change orders, delays, and avoidable rework on site.
  • Site teams working from outdated or superseded drawings, leading to construction errors, RFIs, and loss of confidence in project information.
  • Manual quantity take-offs from 2D drawings during tendering, increasing pricing uncertainty, contingency loading, and exposure to cost overruns.
  • Inconsistent setup and use of Dalux Field, resulting in fragmented QA/QC documentation that does not meet handover or ICT Order requirements.
Key Performance Metrics
  • Cost variance against tender price — measuring commercial predictability and margin protection.
  • Number of clashes identified and resolved prior to site mobilisation — indicating design maturity and coordination effectiveness.
  • RFI volume and average response time — reflecting information quality and project communication efficiency.
  • Rework hours and associated cost (DKK per m²) — capturing the direct impact of coordination and information issues on site productivity.
  • ICT Order compliance score at handover — assessing the completeness, quality, and usability of delivered project information.

Municipalities

Schools, care homes, roads, sports facilities, public infrastructure
ICT Order · Danish Building Authority · GIS-BIM · Civil 3D
Municipalities are Denmark's largest public building clients — legally obligated to comply with the ICT Order (IKT-bekendtgørelsen) on state-subsidized projects, yet procurement teams rarely have technical competence to write enforceable requirements or evaluate contractor deliverables. The gap between legal requirements and what ends up in the asset register is enormous. BIM & ICT advisory closes that gap from both sides — procurement and delivery.
Municipality-Specific Pain Points
  • Employer's Information Requirements (EIR) reused from generic templates without effective enforcement, validation, or audit mechanisms, resulting in non-compliant project deliveries and limited data usability at handover.
  • GIS and BIM systems operating in silos across municipal departments (planning, roads, utilities, buildings, operations), preventing a shared understanding of assets and increasing coordination effort and decision-making risk.
  • Inconsistent asset data quality across the municipal portfolio, with varying levels of completeness, accuracy, and structure depending on project, contractor, or delivery year.
  • Lack of municipal standards for digital documentation of buildings and infrastructure, leading to fragmented data formats, unclear ownership, and limited reuse of information across projects and lifecycle stages.
  • Inaccurate or incomplete mapping of underground infrastructure (pipes, cables, and ducts), increasing the risk of utility strikes, service disruptions, safety incidents, and unplanned maintenance costs.
  • Limited linkage between capital project data and operational systems, meaning valuable project information is not effectively transferred to maintenance, permitting, and asset management workflows.
Key Municipal Performance Metrics
  • Percentage of projects compliant with the ICT Order (IKT-bekendtgørelsen) at audited handover, demonstrating regulatory compliance and effectiveness of information governance.
  • Asset data completeness and consistency across the municipal portfolio, measured by defined data standards, attribute coverage, and spatial accuracy thresholds.
  • Budget and schedule adherence on capital projects, reflecting improved predictability enabled by better information management and coordination.
  • Spatial accuracy of underground infrastructure records in GIS, including confidence levels for pipes, cables, and ducts, supporting safer excavation and planning activities.
  • Annual reduction in maintenance backlog (DKK/year) attributable to improved asset information, prioritised interventions, and data-driven maintenance planning.

Engineering Consultants

Architects, structural & MEP engineers, surveyors, landscape architects
ICT Order · ISO 19650-2 · IFC · BIM Coordination
Danish advisory firms increasingly win commissions requiring credible BIM methodology but few have a dedicated BIM specialist in-house. The result: generic BEPs that don't reflect the project, federated models no one actively manages, and QA failures that damage client relationships. An embedded specialist raises the firm's delivery standard without adding permanent headcount.
Consultant-Specific Pain Points
  • BIM methodology sections in tenders written without hands-on modelling experience, resulting in weak, non-credible commitments that are difficult to deliver and defend during project execution.
  • Lack of active coordination of the federated model, allowing clashes to accumulate between disciplines and only surface late, increasing redesign effort and reputational risk.
  • Inconsistent modelling standards and templates across the project team, leading to incompatible models and inefficient coordination workflows.
  • Unreliable or incomplete IFC deliveries, meaning clients cannot effectively use the models they were contractually promised, exposing the consultant to audit findings and rework.
  • Limited use of automation for repetitive modelling tasks, causing senior engineers and specialists to spend excessive time on low-value administrative work.
Key Consultant Performance Metrics
  • Tender win rate on commissions with BIM and ICT requirements, reflecting credibility and competitiveness in digitally enabled procurements.
  • Clash count identified at coordination reviews, indicating the effectiveness of interdisciplinary coordination and model quality control.
  • Client ICT Order (IKT-bekendtgørelsen) audit pass rate, demonstrating compliance and reliability of information deliverables.
  • Hours spent on repetitive modelling and documentation tasks, highlighting efficiency and use of specialist resources.
  • Repeat commission rate from satisfied clients, capturing long-term trust and perceived delivery quality.

Document Management for Contractors

Site drawing control, submittal tracking, QA documentation, handover packages
ICT Order · Dalux Build · ISO 19650-2
On complex construction projects, poor document control leads to costly rework, delays, and disputes. When teams work from outdated drawings or incomplete information, the impact escalates quickly across trades and timelines. Contractors need document control that is fast, field-proof, and audit-ready — not a shared drive that no one manages. Structured document management from contract award through to handover protects programme, budget, and against disputes.
Critical Risks
  • Trade teams working from superseded or unapproved drawing revisions, causing construction errors and avoidable rework.
  • No single source of truth — multiple document versions in circulation, creating duplication, confusion, and conflicting instructions.
  • Manual or inconsistent submittal and transmittal tracking, causing approval delays and lost communications.
  • Incomplete or unstructured QA and compliance documentation, failing client audits and exposing the contractor to claims.
  • Last-minute assembly of handover documentation, resulting in client rejection, delays to final payment, and commercial exposure.
What Gets Measured
  • Number of drawing revision errors leading to rework or delays.
  • Submittal and approval cycle time (days).
  • Percentage of documents compliant with naming and classification standards.
  • RFI and technical query response time.
  • Handover readiness index — completeness of O&M, as-built, and asset data at practical completion.

ICT Order compliant project archives, public accountability trails, portfolio document standards
ICT Order · Danish Public Administration Law · Danish Building Authority
Municipal construction projects require documentation that can withstand audits, legal scrutiny, and long-term asset management. Without consistent standards and structured systems, fragmented records create significant governance risks. Document management for municipalities is both a project delivery tool and a governance instrument — ensuring compliant handovers, transparent procurement records, and accessible digital archives that support both project delivery and long-term portfolio management.
Critical Risks
  • No consistent document standards across projects, leading to fragmented information and incomparable portfolio data.
  • Procurement and decision records not structured for audit or legal review, exposing the municipality to accountability challenges.
  • Handover documentation not verified against ICT Order (IKT-bekendtgørelsen) requirements before acceptance, resulting in non-compliant project records.
  • Lack of accessible digital archives for legacy buildings, preventing efficient renovation planning and operational management.
  • Loss of institutional knowledge due to staff turnover and poor information governance, creating organisational risk.
What Gets Measured
  • Percentage of projects compliant with ICT Order (IKT-bekendtgørelsen) at handover.
  • Completeness of procurement and decision audit trails.
  • Percentage of portfolio assets with accessible digital documentation.
  • Compliance with document retention policies (Forvaltningsloven).
  • Number of non-compliance issues identified during audits or public inquiries.
